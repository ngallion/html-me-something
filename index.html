Tea Bagging

Since a young age, I have enjoyed the fruits of the revered herb Camellia Sinensis. Up until age 20, I knew it only as the crushed-up leaves in a piece of folded-up and stapled filter paper (tea bag). My first favorite tea was the ever-popular Earl Grey, which I would brew in a coffee cup in the microwave (for one minute) and add roughly 25-50% cold milk, and almost as much granulated sugar.

I dabbled with other types--ginger to ease the stomach, green for a light taste, and various other fruit infused varieties. But, for the first part of my life, when I thought of tea, I imagined the taste of sugary, milky earl grey. They were simpler times.

The Reign of Coffee

Beginning sometime around age 18, I was introduced to the miraculous wonders of the coffee bean. While coffee is certainly not tea, I would consider it a stop on my "voyage" into tea. I began with sugared up Starbucks concoctions, but gradually descended into home brewing--the deeper I descended, my cups slowly turned darker and darker, till I was drinking pure, unadulturated black bean water (which is still my preference). Eventually I found myself making americanos with an Aeropress every morning before work. Coffee holds a special place in my heart, but I've found that it is quite the double-edged sword. Caffeine dependency is no joke. I found myself relying too much on its abilities to wake me up, so I decided to take a break and pursue other, less potent forms of alertness enablers.
